Deliciously Spiced – Minced Meat Kebab

Minced Kebab
Prep
1 hr

This kebab recipe made with spiced beef or lamb mince is tender, juicy, and packed with flavor. Perfect for grilling or pan-frying, it’s a classic loved all across the Middle East and South Asia.

🍖 Ingredients:

  • Beef/Mutton/Lamb mince – 1 kg (70% meat, 30% fat)
  • Red bell pepper – 1 medium (finely chopped)
  • Garlic cloves – 5–6 (minced)
  • Onion – 1 large (grated and squeezed to remove water)
  • Parsley or Coriander – ½ bunch or ½ cup chopped
  • Salt – ¾ tbsp or to taste
  • Black pepper – 1 tsp
  • Sumac – 1 tbsp (or use 1 tbsp anardana powder or 2 tbsp lemon juice with a little zest)
  • Coriander powder – ½ tbsp
  • Cumin powder – 1 tsp
  • Red chilli powder – 2 tsp
  • Paprika – 1 tsp
  • Oil – 2–3 tbsp for frying or brushing on grill

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Prepare the ingredients: Finely chop the red bell pepper, mince garlic, and grate onion (squeeze out excess water to avoid sogginess).
  2. Mix everything: In a large bowl, combine mince, red bell pepper, garlic, onion, parsley/coriander, salt, pepper, sumac, and all spices.
  3. Knead well: Mix and knead the mixture using your hands for 5–10 minutes until the mixture becomes sticky and binds well.
  4. Rest the mix: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30–60 minutes. This helps the flavors develop and the kebabs hold shape.
  5. Shape the kebabs: Wet your hands, then form the mixture into kebab shapes (cylinder or patties). You can use skewers if grilling.
  6. Cook: Heat oil in a pan or grill. Place kebabs gently and cook on medium-high for 4–5 minutes each side until fully cooked and golden.
  7. Optional grilling: You can grill the kebabs over charcoal for smoky flavor or cook in the oven at 200°C (390°F) for 20–25 mins.
  8. Serve hot with naan, rice, salad, and garlic yogurt dip or hummus.

💡 Pro Tips:

  • Use fatty mince – It keeps the kebabs moist and juicy.
  • Remove moisture – Squeeze onions to avoid soggy kebabs that fall apart.
  • Chill the mixture – It helps the kebabs hold shape better during cooking.
  • Sumac or lemon adds brightness – don’t skip this ingredient!
  • Wet hands before shaping to avoid sticking.
